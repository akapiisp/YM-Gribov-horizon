We study the effect of Gribov horizon on confinement and mass gap in Yang-Mills. We study this by  deforming the Yang-Mills action via the smallest eigenvalue of the gauge potential. This introduces a continuous deformation parameter s that characterizes how close to the Gribov horizon the path-integral samples. It seems plausible that only the proximity of the horizon matters for confinement and gap. At it's current stage the deformation is gauge-variant. It would be interesting to have a gauge-invariant analogous mechanism.
